{% sectionCopy eyebrow="individuals" title="health data, productivity, and everything between." %}
Health is where most people start. Lab results sitting in email, sleep data on a watch, a nutrition log that never lasted. The system connects all of it into one private AI that tracks trends across years of data, messages you with daily briefings, and flags when something needs attention.

It usually expands from there. The same system triages email, manages calendar, tracks a Tokyo apartment search, reminds you about an expiring passport. Adding a capability means connecting a data source to the private AI layer, not signing up for another subscription.

Most of our individual clients are in Bangkok. Their systems run on a Mac Mini at home, syncing health data from an Apple Watch, ingesting lab results from local clinics, and planning meals from what is in the fridge.

{% sectionCopy eyebrow="businesses" title="firms, clinics, and teams with scattered data." %}
A Bangkok law firm has twenty years of contracts on a shared drive nobody searches. The private AI system indexes the archive and makes it queryable in plain language. "Find all non-compete clauses from supplier contracts signed in 2023" returns results in seconds from 140,000 documents on the firm's own server.

A dental practice processes the same insurance forms, medical histories, and consent documents dozens of times a day. The system reads them, extracts the fields, flags inconsistencies, and routes completed packages to the practitioner. Patient records stay on the practice's hardware with every access logged.

A construction company tracks permits across multiple sites, coordinates subcontractors through four channels, and manages procurement deadlines that slip when someone misses an email. The system pulls it into one surface: permit status from scanned documents, subcontractor updates from email and messaging, deadlines from contracts.

These are different data sources plugged into the same architecture. The private AI layer, built on OpenClaw, handles orchestration. Each business gets a system configured for how they actually work.
{% /sectionCopy %}

{% sectionCopy eyebrow="every deployment" title="what ships with every system." tone="subtle" %}
Every system ships with private AI models running on your hardware, encrypted storage with audit logging, and data ingestion from email, messaging, and webhooks. You interact through the tools you already use: Telegram, Signal, email, whatever your team is on.

No cloud APIs touch your data unless you explicitly choose that for a specific task. Most clients never need it.

The system is legible. You can read the configuration, audit the logs, and trace every action. Cancel any time, unplug the hardware, and everything is still yours.
