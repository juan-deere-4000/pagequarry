{% sectionCopy eyebrow="individuals" title="personal AI: health, productivity, everything." %}
Health is the most common starting point. You have a wearable, years of bloodwork PDFs sitting in email, maybe a food log you abandoned. The system connects all of it. Lab results come in from your clinic, sleep and activity sync from your watch, you log meals through a message.

It rarely stays just health. The same system triages your email, manages your calendar, tracks a property search, reminds you about your expiring passport. Adding a capability means connecting a data source, not buying another subscription.

{% sectionCopy eyebrow="legal and professional" title="firms and consultants with years of documents." tone="subtle" %}
A law firm has twenty years of contracts in a shared drive nobody searches. The system indexes the archive and makes it searchable in plain language. "Find all non-compete clauses from supplier contracts signed in 2023" returns results in seconds.

From there: deadline extraction, renewal tracking, pre-meeting briefs from case history, client intake that routes documents without someone reading and forwarding every email. Data stays on the firm's server. No client documents pass through external APIs.

Consulting firms get the same architecture for research, report drafting, and knowledge management across engagements. The specifics change. The system does not.
{% /sectionCopy %}

{% sectionCopy eyebrow="healthcare" title="clinics and practices with sensitive patient data." %}
Patient intake is where the system earns its keep fastest. A dental practice processes the same insurance forms, medical histories, and consent documents dozens of times a day. The system reads them, extracts the fields, flags inconsistencies, routes completed packages to the practitioner.

For clinics: appointment optimization, follow-up automation, treatment plan tracking, clinical note assistance.

Patient records stay on the practice's hardware. No intermediary, no third-party cloud. Every action logged, every access traceable.
{% /sectionCopy %}

{% sectionCopy eyebrow="operations" title="businesses that run on coordination." tone="subtle" %}
A construction company tracks permits across multiple projects, coordinates subs through four channels, manages procurement timelines that slip when someone misses an email. The system pulls it into one surface: permit status from documents, sub updates from email and messaging, deadlines from contracts, daily reports from field notes.

Restaurants, hotels, real estate agencies all have their version of the same problem: data scattered across platforms that never talk to each other. Same system, different configuration.
{% /sectionCopy %}

{% sectionCopy eyebrow="every deployment" title="the shared foundation." %}
Every system ships with the same base: private AI models on your hardware, encrypted storage with audit logging, OpenClaw orchestration, data ingestion from email, messaging, and webhooks. You interact through Telegram, Signal, email, whatever you already use.

The system is legible. You can read the configuration and audit the logs. If you stop using it, unplug the hardware and everything is still yours.
