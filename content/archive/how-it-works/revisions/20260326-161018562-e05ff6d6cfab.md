{% sectionCopy eyebrow="the system" title="one layer across everything you already use." %}
You already have email, calendars, file storage, health apps. None of them share information with each other.

The system is a private AI layer built on OpenClaw that connects those sources. Your email stays in your email. Your documents stay in your document storage. The AI reads from where they already live and acts across them in a single turn.

A law firm's contracts become searchable in plain language. A clinic's intake forms get processed and routed to the right practitioner. Personal health data from years of bloodwork, sleep tracking, and nutrition logging gets trend analysis and daily briefings. Different data, same architecture.

{% linkItem href="/services" label="what we build" summary="The same system configured for individuals, practices, and businesses." /%}
{% /sectionCopy %}

{% sectionCopy eyebrow="privacy" title="your data stays on your hardware." tone="subtle" %}
AI models run on a machine you physically control: a server in your Bangkok office, a Mac Mini in your home, a device we configure and hand over. Documents, emails, patient records, and contracts stay local. Nothing routes through external APIs unless you explicitly choose that for a specific task.

This is the architecture, not a policy. A dental practice's patient records stay on the practice's server. A family office's financials stay on the family office's machine. There is no intermediary and no third-party processor. Most clients never need a cloud model.

If a task does benefit from a cloud model, the private AI system lets you choose per-task. You see exactly what data goes where.
{% /sectionCopy %}

{% sectionCopy eyebrow="ownership" title="start with one thing. keep everything." %}
There is no dashboard to sign into and no app to download. The private AI system lives on hardware you control, and you interact through tools you already use: Telegram, Signal, email, whatever your team is on.

Most clients start with one pain point. A firm wants to search its contract archive. A person wants to track their health data. A Bangkok clinic wants to fix intake paperwork. We build that first. Expanding later means connecting another data source, not starting a new project.

Cancel any time. Unplug the hardware and everything is still there. The system, the data, and every configuration file are yours.
