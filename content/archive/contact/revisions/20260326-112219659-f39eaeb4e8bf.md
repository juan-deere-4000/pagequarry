{% sectionCopy eyebrow="what to bring" title="a problem, not a shopping list." %}
you do not need to know what you want built. you need to know what is costing you time, what is fragmented, or what keeps falling through cracks.

a managing partner who spends three hours before every client meeting pulling together relevant history from emails and old files. a clinic owner who watches the front desk re-enter the same patient information into three systems every morning. someone with five years of lab results in email attachments they have never compared side by side. a project manager tracking permits across four active sites in a spreadsheet that is always out of date.

the conversation starts with the bottleneck. the architecture comes after.
{% /sectionCopy %}

{% sectionCopy eyebrow="what happens" title="scope first, then a proposal if it fits." tone="subtle" %}
the first conversation is a working session. we look at what tools you use, where the friction is, and what a minimum useful system looks like for your situation. sometimes the right scope is one capability. sometimes it is a connected set of three.

the system is built on OpenClaw, an open-source ai agent framework, and deployed on hardware you control. if the model fits your situation, we put together a proposal: what gets built, what it connects to, how long it takes. if it does not fit, we will say so.
{% /sectionCopy %}
