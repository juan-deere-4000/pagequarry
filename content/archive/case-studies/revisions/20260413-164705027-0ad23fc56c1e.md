{% sectionCopy eyebrow="Why Proof Matters" title="The Product Should Show Its Work." %}
PageQuarry makes a strong claim: humans and AI agents can publish faster when the design system stays in code and content flows through approved blocks. That claim needs visible proof, not just feature language.

The pages here are meant to show the workflow, the constraints, and the resulting editorial surface in public.

{% linkItem href="/case-studies/teams/community-knowledge-base" label="How PageQuarry.com Ships Itself" summary="See how the public site runs on the same publishing model it describes." /%}
{% /sectionCopy %}

{% sectionCopy eyebrow="What to Look For" title="Use the Proof Pages to Judge the Workflow, Not the Marketing." tone="subtle" %}
Look for what stays in code, what moves through markdown, and how much of the site can change without reopening layout decisions.

- One repo instead of a split marketing stack
- Accepted content visible in the repo
- Publishing flow shared by humans and agents
- Supporting infrastructure kept out of the writing path
{% /sectionCopy %}
